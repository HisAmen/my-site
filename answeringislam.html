Section 01 — Lineage

🧬 Ishmael Was Never the Chosen Line Genesis 17 · Covenant theology

Islam teaches that Ishmael — son of Abraham by Hagar — was the true heir of the Abrahamic covenant and the ancestor of Muhammad and the Arab people. The claim is foundational to the Islamic identity: if Ishmael is the chosen line, Islam is the legitimate continuation of the Abrahamic faith.

But the Bible doesn't just leave this ambiguous. God himself makes the answer explicit — and He makes it in the exact moment Ishmael comes up:

Genesis 17:18–21 (KJV)

"And Abraham said unto God, O that Ishmael might live before thee! And God said, Sarah thy wife shall bear thee a son indeed; and thou shalt call his name Isaac: and I will establish my covenant with him for an everlasting covenant, and with his seed after him. And as for Ishmael, I have heard thee: Behold, I have blessed him, and will make him fruitful, and will multiply him exceedingly; twelve princes shall he beget, and I will make him a great nation. But my covenant will I establish with Isaac, which Sarah shall bear unto thee at this set time in the next year."

Let's read that carefully. Abraham — visibly emotional — literally pleads for Ishmael to be included. He says, "O that Ishmael might live before thee!" And God's answer isn't vague. It is a direct, explicit denial: "But my covenant will I establish with Isaac."

The theological problem for Islam:

According to Islamic sources, Ishmael is the direct ancestor of the Arab peoples and of the Prophet Muhammad himself (see Ibn Hisham, Sirat Rasul Allah, the earliest biography of Muhammad, which traces his genealogy through Ishmael → Adnan → Arab tribes → Abdullah → Muhammad). Yet Genesis 17 shows that Ishmael's existence was not the plan — he was conceived when Abraham and Sarah took matters into their own hands (Genesis 16) rather than waiting for God's promise. Isaac was the covenant child — born supernaturally to a barren woman (Genesis 21:1-2).

Ishmael is never said to receive the prophetic gift, the Torah, the Psalms, or the line of kings. The Scripture records no prophets descending from Ishmael. The entire prophetic tradition — from Isaac to Jacob to Moses to David to Isaiah to Jeremiah — flows through Isaac's line.
Genesis 21:12 (KJV)

"And God said unto Abraham, Let it not be grievous in thy sight because of the lad, and because of thy bondwoman; in all that Sarah hath said unto thee, hearken unto her voice; for in Isaac shall thy seed be called."

The Apostle Paul makes this covenant argument explicit in Galatians 4, calling Hagar a figure of bondage (law) and Sarah a figure of freedom (grace). The child of the promise is Isaac, not Ishmael. The prophetic, covenant, and messianic lineage in Scripture uniformly and exclusively passes through Isaac → Jacob → the twelve tribes → David → Jesus.

📌 The lineage of prophecy:
Abraham (covenant) → Isaac (Genesis 17:21) → Jacob/Israel (Genesis 35:10) → Judah (Genesis 49:10, "the scepter shall not depart from Judah") → David (2 Samuel 7:12-16) → Jesus (Matthew 1:1, "Jesus Christ, son of David, son of Abraham")

❌ Ishmael → No prophets. No covenant. No Psalms. No Torah. No Messiah.

The Islamic claim requires that the entire Hebrew prophetic tradition got the lineage backwards — that Allah's chosen line was Ishmael but that centuries of prophets all taught the opposite. This creates an extraordinary burden of proof. The Qur'an itself even claims to confirm the Torah and the Gospel (we'll get to that). You cannot simultaneously confirm those books and overturn their most explicit covenant promise.

Section 03 — Textual Integrity

The Qur'an has not one version but multiple distinct textual traditions called qira'at (readings). There are at minimum ten accepted qira'at, each with its own chain of transmission, its own voweling, and in many cases its own different words.

Currently used distinct Qur'anic text traditions:

Hafs — Muslim world in general (most common)
Warsh — Algeria, Morocco, West Africa, parts of Sudan
Qalun — Libya, Tunisia, parts of Qatar
Al-Duri — Parts of Sudan and West Africa
Hisham — Parts of Yemen

Source: Abu Ammaar Yasir Qadhi, An Introduction to the Sciences of the Qur'an, 1999, p. 199.

— Sahih al-Bukhari, Volume 6, Book 61, Number 510 (Narrated Anas bin Malik)

Why would you burn manuscripts of the "perfectly preserved" word of God? The Islamic answer — that he was standardizing, not suppressing — cannot explain why existing valid Qur'ans needed to be destroyed rather than simply retired. Burning is not archiving. Burning is suppression.

This happened approximately 20 years after Muhammad's death. Ibn Mas'ud, notably, refused to surrender his copy and publicly rejected Uthman's version, saying it differed materially from what he had received directly from the Prophet.

— Reported in Musnad Ahmad; see also Itqan fi 'Ulum al-Qur'an by al-Suyuti

Contrast this with the New Testament. We have over 5,800 Greek manuscripts of the New Testament, many predating Muhammad. Textual scholars can identify variants, trace them, and reconstruct the original text with a reliability no other ancient document approaches. No Christian authority ever burned manuscript variants. The New Testament's transmission is demonstrably more transparent, more documented, and more verifiable than the Qur'an's.

Section 06 — Sexual Ethics

💍 Mut'ah — Islam's Temporary Marriage Sahih Muslim · sexual contract

Mut'ah (Arabic: متعة — "pleasure") is a practice of contracted temporary marriage. A man and a woman agree to a marriage for a fixed period — hours, days, or weeks — in exchange for a payment (mahr) to the woman. When the period ends, the marriage automatically dissolves. No divorce required. No ongoing obligation.

Qur'an 4:24 (An-Nisa) — the textual basis

"And also forbidden to you are all married women except those your right hands possess. This is the decree of Allah for you. Beyond these, it is lawful for you to seek [in marriage] with your wealth in a proper manner, desiring chastity, not unlawful sexual intercourse. So for whatever you enjoy [of marriage] from them, give them their due compensation as an obligation. And there is no blame upon you for what you mutually agree to beyond the obligation. Indeed, Allah is ever Knowing and Wise."

Hadith confirmation:

"We used to participate in the holy battles led by Allah's Messenger and we had no women with us. So we said, 'Shall we castrate ourselves?' But the Prophet forbade us to do that and thenceforth he allowed us to marry a woman temporarily by giving her even a garment, and then he recited: 'O you who believe! Do not make unlawful the good things which Allah has made lawful for you.'" — Sahih al-Bukhari, Book 62, Hadith 13 (Narrated Abdullah)

Sahih Muslim 1404b: "Allah's Messenger permitted us to contract temporary marriage for three nights." — Narrated Salama bin Al-Akwa

Mut'ah is still practiced today in Shia Islam, where it is called sigheh in Iran. While mainstream Sunni Islam later declared it forbidden (citing a separate hadith where it was prohibited), the critical point is that it was explicitly practiced by Muhammad and permitted under his direct authority. You cannot say it was fabricated by later followers — it is in Sahih Bukhari and Sahih Muslim.

What this is in plain terms:

Strip away the religious terminology. A man pays a woman a sum. They agree to be "married" for a set number of hours or days. They have sexual relations. The arrangement ends. The woman may remarry the same way with a different man.

In any secular legal framework, this is a sexual transaction — what in the West would be called prostitution, given that the defining characteristic (payment exchanged for sexual access for a defined period, with no lasting commitment) is identical. The Islamic framework gives it a religious label, but the structure is identical.

Jesus, by contrast, elevated marriage to a covenant image of God's love for His people — permanent, sacrificial, and exclusive (Matthew 19:4-6). Paul called the marriage relationship an image of Christ and the Church (Ephesians 5:25-32). The contrast in sexual ethics between the two systems could not be more stark.

Islam's claim is essentially this: for six centuries, the entire Christian world — scholars, martyrs, bishops, missionaries, councils — preserved a corrupted gospel. They all got it wrong. Then Muhammad, an illiterate Arab merchant, received the correction from an angel.

Consider what this requires you to believe:

→ The disciples of Jesus — who watched him die and claimed to watch him rise — taught a lie and died for it.

→ And yet the Qur'an simultaneously tells Muhammad to consult these same scriptures when in doubt (Qur'an 10:94).

These claims cannot all be simultaneously true.

Christianity doesn't just predate Islam historically — it has a documented chain of eyewitness testimony going back to the first generation. Paul writes to the Corinthians c. 55 AD that Jesus "appeared to Peter, then to the twelve. After that, he appeared to more than five hundred brothers and sisters at the same time, most of whom are still living" (1 Corinthians 15:5-6). He is inviting the audience to check. Five hundred living witnesses, named in a verifiable letter. Islam's founding revelation, by contrast, occurred in a private cave with no witnesses except Muhammad's own account.

Section 09 — The Hadith Record

📜 Problematic Hadith Traditions Canonical sources · Bukhari · Muslim

The following are not obscure traditions buried in discredited texts. These appear in Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim — the two most authoritative hadith collections in Sunni Islam, whose very name Sahih means "authentic."

📌 Muhammad and the Magic Spell (Bukhari 5765):

Narrated Aisha: "Allah's Messenger was bewitched so that he used to think that he had done a thing which in fact he had not done." A spell was cast on Muhammad by a Jewish man named Labid ibn al-Asam using a comb, a piece of hair, and a pollen spathe of a male date palm. Muhammad was under the delusion that he had done things he had not done — for up to a year. He was eventually shown the location of the spell in a vision and it was removed.

This is a profound theological problem: the man receiving divine revelation was himself vulnerable to being deceived through witchcraft. If his perception of reality was manipulable by a Jewish magician, how does one verify any revelation received during that period?
📌 Fly Wings and Medicine (Bukhari 3320):

"The Prophet said, 'If a house fly falls in the drink of anyone of you, he should dip it (in the drink), because one of its wings has a disease and the other has the cure for the disease.'"

Modern microbiology establishes that houseflies carry bacteria, fungi, and pathogens on both wings and all surfaces. Immersing a fly deeper into a drink is not a medical remedy — it increases contamination. This tradition, presented as prophetic guidance, is medically false.
📌 The Satanic Verses Incident:

Early Islamic historical sources (al-Tabari, Ibn Ishaq in his Sirat) record that Muhammad temporarily recited verses approving of three pagan Meccan goddesses — Al-Lat, Al-Uzza, and Manat — as intercessors.
