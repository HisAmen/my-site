📡 David Paulides — Bigfoot, DNA & The Interdimensional Connection

110 DNA samples · 5 PhDs · No known match

Source: YouTube Link

David Paulides spent 20 years as a police officer before two tech founders — both with separate Bigfoot encounters in their youth — funded his investigation. He set up on the Hoopa Reservation in Northern California, about 35 miles south of the Patterson-Gimlin film site.

Native American sheriffs at the local substation opened doors for him immediately, connecting him with tribal elders that normally would have taken five to ten years to reach. Over two years, Paulides collected 110 DNA samples including hair, blood, saliva, and tissue. A team of five PhDs across five certified labs ran the analysis.

🧬 The results came back the same across multiple independent samples:

Nuclear DNA on the male side has no known match in GenBank.

On the female side, mitochondrial DNA traces back roughly 12,000 years to the Middle East.

GenBank contacted the team asking if they had discovered an unknown tribe in the Amazon.

Paulides also tested 58 samples from elongated Peruvian skulls. The mitochondrial DNA came back Middle Eastern. Carbon-14 dating placed them around 800 years old — centuries before Columbus. Multiple labs ran the same samples blind and got the same results. No contamination.
