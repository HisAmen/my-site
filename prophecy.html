🎯 Brandon's Prophecy — Trump Assassination Attempt

Predicted March 14 · Happened July 13 · Shot in ear

Source: YouTube Link

On July 13, 2024, Thomas Crooks climbed a roof in Butler, Pennsylvania, and fired multiple rounds. One bullet grazed Trump's ear. An inch to the right and it would have been a headshot. Trump survived.

Think about this. Four months before. Specific detail about the ear. No known inside information. No connection to the shooter. Just a prediction. And it happened exactly as described.

Even drones will be used to detect who has the mark and who doesn't. People won't be able to buy or sell. They will have to go off-grid to survive. Whether you believe that or not, the accuracy of the first prediction makes you stop and think.

🏛️ Specific Prophecies — Tyre · Cyrus · Isaiah 53 · Zechariah 12:10

Fulfilled to the letter
📍 Ezekiel 26 (Tyre Prophecy) — odds ~1 in 75 million

Ezekiel predicted in 587 or 586 BC that the mainland city of Tyre would be destroyed and its rubble thrown into the sea. Alexander the Great did exactly that in 332 BC. He destroyed the mainland city, used the rubble to build a causeway to the island half a mile offshore, then destroyed that city too. Every detail fulfilled.
📍 Isaiah 45:1 (Cyrus) — named 150 years in advance

Around 700 BC, Isaiah did something a fortune cookie never does. He got specific. He named a man — Cyrus — who would free the Jewish exiles and order Jerusalem's temple rebuilt. He even said Cyrus would subdue nations and open gates that would not be shut. That's exactly how the Persian army entered Babylon. Isaiah called Cyrus the LORD's "anointed" — language reserved for Israel's own kings. This prophecy was written 150 years before Cyrus was even born.
📍 Isaiah 53 (The Suffering Servant) — pierced for our transgressions

Written 700 years before Jesus. It describes a man who would be despised and rejected, wounded for our transgressions, bruised for our iniquities. He would be led like a lamb to the slaughter. He would be pierced. He would be buried with the rich. And he would bear the sin of many. The Jewish scribes who preserved this text never understood it until they saw Jesus. Then it all made sense.
📍 Zechariah 12:10 — they will look upon Me whom they have pierced

Zechariah records God Himself speaking: "They shall look upon Me whom they have pierced." YHWH says they will pierce Him. When Jesus was crucified, John writes that this verse was fulfilled (John 19:37). They pierced Jesus. They pierced God. And then — here is the kicker — the verse says they will mourn for Him as one mourns for an only son. That is exactly what happened after the crucifixion. The early church grew out of that mourning turning to joy.

These are not vague horoscope readings. These are specific names, specific cities, specific methods of death, written centuries before they happened. And every single one came true.
